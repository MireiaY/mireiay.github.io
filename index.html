Projects

Algorithmic zoning project

The idea behind the “Algorithmic Zoning” initiative is to explore dynamically reconfigurable incentives for the pro-social development of cities. As part of the CityScope team – a data-driven tangible platform that helps different stakeholders shape the most favorable urban scenario – I developed a first version of the pro-social incentive module. I deployed Agent-Based Modeling –GAMA platform– and Machine Learning techniques –Python– to define citizens behavioral patterns regarding mobility mode and housing choice, which were calibrated using transportation and census data in the Greater Boston Area, with a special focus on Kendall Square. This computational model included various financial incentives that aimed to address the deficiency of affordable housing in the area. The consequences that these financial incentives would entail were monitored.

Design and implementation of an industrial safety system for the storage and extraction of steel profiles

Design and development of the operation scheme of an industrial safety system for storing and extracting steel profiles. In this project, as part of a four-member team, I dentified and implemented the necessary electronic elements –sensors, processing modules, actuators, user interfaces— for the correct operation and control of the mentioned industrial process. I analyzed different what-if scenarios to determine potential risks of the procedure and I suggested a security protocol. A small-scale prototype was also built with physical electronic elements and the myRIO platform –National Instruments— and LabView were used to implement the control system of the electronic architecture.



Structural design of an industrial estate and evaluation of the necessary amenities

Design of a real industrial estate to meet the requirements of the Spanish norm. As part of a four-member team, I calculated and sized the principal structural elements of the estate, considering the maximum forces and deformations that each element could withstand. In the second part of the project the lighting, ventilation and fire protection systems of this same industrial estate were designed.



Design of a CNC machining center for the automation of the production of coupling elements

This project consisted in carrying out a productivity study of the current manufacturing process of coupling elements and suggesting a preliminary design of an alternative machining center. First, an analysis of the required machining conditions was held, so as to produce 150,000 coupling elements a year with the given geometric requirements. The preliminary design of the machine was then carried out following the eleven principles of precision engineering. This meant defining the layout, the structure, the supports, guiding elements, drives, CNC control and engine of the machining center. We also performed an economic analysis of the proposal.



Design and prototyping of a rotatory lifting device

The main objective of this project was the design and manufacturing of a rotatory lifting device prototype, which had to be able to elevate a 1 kg cylinder and make it spin 90º and 180º in the two axes (x,y) orthogonal to the axis defining its own rotation (z). The development process consisted of a preliminary design step, followed by a force analysis phase. Once I defined the actuating forces along with my three teammates, I calculated stresses and deformations in each piece using the Finite Element Method. The last step consisted in manufacturing the pieces using 3D Printers and programming the corresponding movements using Arduino.
